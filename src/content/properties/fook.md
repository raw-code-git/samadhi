FOOK is a Chinese kitchen that didn't stop evolving in 1985. The room is unmistakable — deep lacquered red, polished brass, a single long counter facing the fire — but the menu moves with the times.

Wood-fired roasts come out of a custom oven that runs at 380 °C on lychee wood: char siu that caramelises where it catches, duck with skin that shatters. The dim sum trolley still circles at lunch. Dinner tilts toward the kitchen's more ambitious work — whole fish steamed with aged ginger, claypot rice with preserved sausage, desserts built around hot-cold contrasts.

Group bookings welcome. Private dining room seats twelve.
