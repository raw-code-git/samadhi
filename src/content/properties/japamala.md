Japamala occupies eleven acres of Tioman's quiet southern coast — a shelf of rainforest that tumbles down to the South China Sea. The chalets are woven into the terrain: some cantilevered out over the canopy, others perched on the cliff with nothing between the bed and the horizon but glass.

Days here move at island time. Breakfast lingers at Barra, the beachfront bar. Afternoons fold into diving, kayaking, or the slow work of doing nothing by the pool. When the sun sets, Than opens its kitchen for a menu of modern Asian cooking, paired with the last of the light.

Tioman is reached by ferry from Mersing. Our concierge handles the logistics.
