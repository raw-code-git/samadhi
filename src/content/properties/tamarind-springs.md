You hear Tamarind Springs before you see it: the rush of the stream through the ravine, the crackle of lanterns, the quiet hum of a full dining room fifty metres from the nearest road.

The cooking follows the Mekong — the sour-salt-sweet balance of Cambodian *amok*, the grilled leaves of Vietnamese *bò lá lốt*, the toasted rice in a Laotian *laab*. It is a menu built for slow evenings and shared plates, drawn from recipes our chefs learned in Phnom Penh, Luang Prabang and Hanoi.

Open-air; umbrellas at the ready if the weather turns. Dinner only.
