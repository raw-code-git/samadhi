Tamarind Hill sits inside a 1930s black-and-white bungalow on the slope above Jalan Sultan Ismail — a pocket of shade in the middle of the city. The restaurant has been serving Northern Thai cooking here since 2002.

The menu follows the roads of the old Lanna kingdom: the curries of Chiang Mai, the grilled meats of the Shan plateau, the pickled and fermented flavours that thread through Burmese cooking. Much of what we serve is slow work — braises that take a day, curries built on pastes pounded that morning.

The verandah seating is the quiet room. Book it for dinner.
